## 1. Market Intelligence

### 1.1 Nigeria Proxy Market Overview

**Market Size Estimate:**  
Nigeria's digital advertising, e-commerce, and data scraping markets consume an estimated ₦500M–₦1B annually in proxy services — driven by:

1. **E-commerce verification** — Jumia, Konga, Amazon.ng sellers need clean IPs for account management
2. **Ad fraud detection** — Agencies verifying spend across placements
3. **Price aggregation** — Travel, ticketing, fintech pricing intelligence
4. **Social media management** — Agencies managing multiple accounts
5. **Research & data scraping** — Academic, market research applications

**Current Market Rate (June 2026):**

| Proxy Type | Market Price (₦) | Notes |
|------------|------------------|-------|
| Residential GB | ₦1,500–₦3,000/GB | Varies by quality tier |
| ISP IP/month | ₦5,000–₦15,000/IP | Shared residential quality |
| Dedicated ISP IP | ₦15,000–₦35,000/IP | Clean, dedicated |
| Datacenter IP | ₦2,000–₦5,000/IP/month | Standard IPv4 |

**Exchange Rate Assumption:** ₦1,750 = $1 USD

### 1.2 Competitive Landscape

**Local Resellers (Nigeria):**
- Limited organized competition — market fragmented among individual resellers
- No major local proxy-as-a-service brands identified
- Opportunity for branded, CAC-registered entity

**International Providers Serving Nigeria:**
- Bright Data — Enterprise pricing, minimal local support
- Smartproxy (now Decodo) — Active but no Nigeria-specific positioning
- Oxylabs — Aggressive marketing but poor IP quality (avoid)

### 1.3 Demand Drivers

| Sector | Use Case | Volume Estimate |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| E-commerce | Account creation, order management | 30% of market |
| Digital marketing | Ad verification, competitor tracking | 25% |
| Travel/Ticketing | Price monitoring, bot mitigation | 20% |
| Financial services | KYC, fraud detection | 15% |
| Other | Research, academic | 10% |
